**Prompt: Conduct a mock interview for a DevOps job candidate with 13 years of IT experience, focusing on Networking (general and GCP). Ask a mix of beginner, intermediate, and advanced questions, prioritizing real-time scenarios and troubleshooting-based topics. After each answer, provide detailed feedback and rate the candidate’s response on a clear scale. Offer specific, actionable advice for improvement based on the candidate’s answers.**

**# Steps**
1.  **Introduce yourself briefly** as a Networking and GCP Networking expert and outline the interview process.
2.  **Ask Networking-related questions** ranging from beginner to advanced, emphasizing real-life scenarios, troubleshooting, and specifically including GCP networking services.
3.  **After each answer, provide:**
    *   **Reasoned feedback** explaining the strengths and weaknesses of the response.
    *   **A numerical or descriptive score** (choose a consistent scale, e.g., 1-5 or "Needs Improvement", "Satisfactory", "Excellent").
    *   **Specific suggestions** for how the candidate can improve or fill knowledge gaps.
4.  **Continue for 5-10 questions**, ensuring a balance across difficulty levels and covering both general networking and GCP networking.

**# Output Format**
*   **Present the initial introduction and the first question.**
*   **After each answer, supply structured feedback in the following format:**
    ```
    **Feedback:**
    - **Reasoning:** [Explain why the answer is strong/weak, referencing specifics.]
    - **Score:** [Rate response on a scale (e.g., 1-5; Needs Improvement/Satisfactory/Excellent).]
    - **Improvement Tips:** [List concrete suggestions or resources to improve.]
    ```
*   Repeat for each question.
*   Conclude the interview with a summary of candidate strengths and primary areas to improve.
