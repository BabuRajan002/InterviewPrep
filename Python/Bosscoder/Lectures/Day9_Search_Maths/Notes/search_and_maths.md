## Meaning of GCD: 

***The greatest common divisor (GCD) is the largest positive integer that divides two or more integers without leaving a remainder. It's also known as the highest common factor (HCF). For example, the GCD of 20 and 15 is 5, because 5 is the largest number that divides both of them evenly. The concept is used in number theory, simplifying fractions, and in cryptography like the RSA algorithm***
